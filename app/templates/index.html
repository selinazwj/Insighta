Our Story

We built this because
we lived the problem.

Every great research project should stand or fall on the quality of its ideas; not on whether the researcher could guilt-trip enough friends into filling out a Google Form.

"We spent two weeks begging people to fill our survey. That's when we knew something was deeply broken."
— The Insighta founding team, BU 2025
01

The Night Before the Deadline

It was 11pm. The survey deadline was tomorrow. We had 23 responses out of the 100 we needed for statistical significance.

We posted in every Facebook group we could find. We texted acquaintances we hadn't spoken to since freshman orientation. We stood outside the dining hall with a laptop, we stick our poster in every bathroom door. It was embarrassing, exhausting, and — if we're being honest — completely normal. Every researcher goes through this.

That night, one of us asked: "Why isn't there a platform that just… connects researchers with people who actually want to participate?"
02

The Other Side of the Problem

As we started talking to more people, we discovered the flip side was just as broken. Hundreds of students were filling out random surveys for course credit or pocket money, but the process was scattered, unreliable, and often felt like their time was being wasted.

We spoke to a sophomore psychology student who had spent three hours on a research participation portal only to realize the survey she completed never properly recorded her response. We talked to a pre-med junior who had no idea that her unique experience with a chronic illness could be exactly the data a researcher across campus was desperately looking for.

The knowledge was there. The willingness was there. The connection was missing.

03

Why We're Different

Existing tools are built for corporations running focus groups with big budgets. They're powerful, yes, but they treat participants as commodities and researchers as just another paying customer. There's no community. No matching. No sense that both sides of the equation actually matter.

Insighta is built from the ground up for the academic and independent research ecosystem: where budgets are small, timelines are tight, and the research actually matters. We're not trying to be another survey tool. We're building the infrastructure for a generation of curious people to actually do their work.

We believe that better research leads to better decisions: in science, in policy, in business. And that starts with making research accessible to everyone who wants to do it.
04

What We're Building Toward

We're still early. We launched as a small team of students at Boston University, and we're building this as we go, listening closely to every researcher and every participant who uses the platform. This is not a venture-backed rocket ship chasing scale. This is a slow, careful build toward something genuinely useful.

Our dream is a world where a first-year student with a fascinating research question has the same access to real participants as a tenured professor with a full lab. Where a curious person can make $20 sharing their authentic experiences and feel like their voice contributed to something meaningful. Where research is a conversation, not a transaction.

Our Journey

How we got here,
and where we're going.

We're a small team building something we genuinely believe in. Here's the honest version of our story.

A Poster in a Bathroom

It started with a survey flyer taped to a bathroom wall: weathered, half-peeling, desperate. Someone had hand-written a URL and the words "Please fill this out!!" underneath. It had clearly been there for weeks. Nobody had filled it out. Looking at that sad little poster, something clicked: this was the state of academic research recruitment in 2025. A student's months of work, reduced to a crumpled piece of paper in a toilet stall that everyone ignored.

We couldn't stop thinking about it.

The Spark
Research

We Interviewed Everyone We Could Find

Before building anything, we went out and talked to people. Psychology undergraduates who needed 100 responses in two weeks. PhD candidates across fields — neuroscience, economics, public health — who had been wrestling with recruitment for months. Professors and industry researchers as well.

The story was the same everywhere. Great research, stuck. Not because the questions weren't good, but because the infrastructure to ask them simply didn't exist.

"That's Too Hard. Don't Do It."

People told us this wouldn't work. Two-sided marketplaces are notoriously difficult to bootstrap — you need participants to attract researchers, and researchers to attract participants. Chicken and egg. Several people we respected told us to pick something simpler. We understood their logic. We did it anyway.

The problem was real, it was affecting people we cared about, and nobody else seemed to be solving it for the academic context specifically. That felt like reason enough to try.

Conviction
BU Innovation

Accepted into BU Innovation Pathway

Our work got noticed. We were accepted into Boston University's Innovation Pathway program: a formal recognition that the problem we were solving was real and worth building toward. It was the first moment the project stopped feeling like a side experiment and started feeling like a real company. We had institutional backing, a structured roadmap, and the confidence to go all in.

The Platform Goes Live

We launched Insighta — a real, working platform where researchers can post surveys and participants can find ones that match their profile and get rewarded for their time. It's not perfect. We're still iterating every week, fixing bugs, adding features, and learning from every researcher and participant who uses it. But it's live, it's real, and it's already connecting people who would never have found each other otherwise.

What We Stand For

Principles, not platitudes.

These aren't values we put on a wall. They're the decisions we make every day about how to build this platform.

Fairness for Both Sides

Every decision we make has to work for both researchers and participants. We refuse to optimize for one at the expense of the other. That's the foundation everything else is built on.

Research Is a Public Good

We believe that good research — on human behavior, health, society, technology — makes the world genuinely better. Making that research easier to do is a cause worth dedicating yourself to.

Transparency Over Hype

We're a small team, we're early, and we're still figuring a lot out. We'd rather be honest about that than pretend we're a fully-formed company with all the answers. Trust is built slowly.

Access Over Prestige

The best research ideas don't only come from elite institutions. We're building tools that work for a first-generation college student with a laptop just as well as they work for a PhD student at a top research university.

Slow and Intentional

We're not racing toward a billion-dollar exit. We're building something that actually works, iterating based on real feedback, and resisting the pressure to grow faster than our quality can support.

Community Over Platform

We want Insighta to feel less like a tool you use and more like a community you belong to — one where researchers and participants genuinely interact, learn from each other, and produce something neither could alone.
